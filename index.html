    About

    Tea, Beverage produced by steeping in freshly boiled water the young leaves and leaf buds of the tea plant, Camellia sinensis, a member of the family Theaceae, which contains 40 genera of trees and shrubs. Tea cultivation is first documented in China in ad 350; according to legend, it had been known there since c.The tea plant The tea plant, Camellia sinensis, is an evergreen shrub or small tree with bright green, shiny leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tea plant also has white, scented flowers that bloom in the autumn. Tea leaf harvesting The best leaves for tea production are the young, light-green leaves with short, white hairs on the underside. The leaves are usually hand-picked every one to two weeks. Tea leaf processing The tea leaf processing steps include: Withering: Reducing moisture to make the leaves pliable Rolling: Breaking down the structures to release essential oils Oxidation: Allowing controlled exposure to air to enhance flavor Drying: Final moisture removal to fix the leaves' properties Tea types The three main types of tea are fermented (black), unfermented (green), and semifermented (oolong or pouchong). Tea quality The quality and flavor profile of the tea is influenced by the tea plant's environment, including the altitude, climate, and soil composition.
